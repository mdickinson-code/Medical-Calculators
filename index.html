Acute PE Clinical Category (A–E)

        
2026 AHA/ACC/ACCP/ACEP/CHEST/SCAI/SHM/SIR/SVM/SVN Guideline — Creager MA et al., Circulation 2026;153(12):e977–e1051

      


      

        
        
      


      
      

        
🩺 Step 1 — Clinical Presentation

        

          
          
        

      


      
      

        
❤️ Step 2 — Hemodynamic Status (most important determinant of Category D/E)

        

          
          
        

        
Category D = incipient failure; Category E = established cardiopulmonary failure or cardiac arrest.

      


      
      

        
📊 Step 3 — Simplified PESI Score (sPESI) 0 pts

        
Each criterion = 1 point. sPESI = 0 → low severity; sPESI ≥ 1 → elevated severity.

        

          
          
          
          
          
          
        

        
sPESI = 0 is one criterion for Category B (outpatient eligibility). sPESI ≥ 1 → Category C minimum if hemodynamically stable.

      


      
      

        
🧪 Step 4 — Biomarkers

        

          

            
            
          

          

            
            
          

          

            
            
          

        

        
Elevated biomarkers without RV dysfunction or hemodynamic compromise → Category C. Lactate elevation may signal Category D/E even if SBP appears preserved.

      


      
      

        
🔬 Step 5 — Right Ventricular Assessment

        

          

            
            
          

          

            
            
          

        

        
RV dysfunction on imaging is a key criterion separating Category C1 (no RV dysfunction) from Category C2 (with RV dysfunction). Significant RV dysfunction combined with biomarker elevation increases urgency of PERT consultation.
